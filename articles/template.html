Article Title Goes Here

May 26, 2026
6 min read

Opening paragraph. This is where the article begins. Set up the problem or the idea immediately — don't ease in with context. The reader decided to keep reading based on the headline; reward that decision in the first sentence.

Second paragraph continues the setup. By the end of the first two paragraphs, the reader should know exactly what they're going to learn and why it matters.

First Section Heading

Section body copy. Keep paragraphs short — three to five sentences maximum. One idea per paragraph.

Pull quotes go here. Use them for the single most important sentence in the article.

Continue the argument or narrative. Each paragraph should earn its place by advancing the point.

Second Section Heading

More body copy. At this point in the article, you're building toward the resolution or recommendation.

  • Bullet points work for lists of parallel items
  • Keep each item to one line where possible
  • Don't use bullets as a substitute for prose

Closing Section

Final section. Land the point. A good closing paragraph tells the reader what to think or do differently now that they've read this.

End with a strong final sentence. Not a summary — a conclusion.
