# StoryArc Strategies — Strategic Priorities

## Current focus areas (update as strategy evolves)

---

### 1. Build getstoryarc.com as a content authority in crypto/fintech content marketing

The blog is the primary channel for demonstrating expertise and attracting inbound.

**What this means for content:**
- Every post should demonstrate something — a framework, a case study, a specific claim — not just describe what we do
- Target: founders, CMOs, and heads of growth at crypto/fintech companies
- The blog should answer the questions they're actually searching for, not questions we wish they were asking

**Key topics to own:**
- Narrative strategy for technical companies
- How to write educational content that builds authority (not just traffic)
- Crypto-specific content strategy (what works in this category, what doesn't)
- The mechanics of institutional communication
- How to build a content function from zero

---

### 2. Establish credibility in the institutional crypto space

**Why:** Our client base (Babylon, Kraken, Bitstamp, institutional protocols) signals that we operate at the serious end of the market. The blog and positioning should reinforce this.

**What this means:**
- Content should not talk down to the reader — assume they understand the technology
- Examples and case studies should be specific and named where possible
- Avoid "intro to crypto" content — that's not our buyer

---

### 3. Drive inbound via search and AI visibility

**Context:** Marketing teams are increasingly asking AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) "who should I use for crypto content strategy?" We need to appear in those answers.

**What this means for content:**
- Articles should be structured to be cited (clear claims, specific data, named mechanisms)
- Cover the questions prospects are asking AI tools in our category
- Build topical depth, not just breadth — LLMs favor sites with genuine expertise concentration

**Priority GSC / ranking targets:**
> Populate from GSC data once blog is live

---

### 4. Demonstrate the AI-native workflow itself

**Why:** One of our unique angles is that we operate an AI-native content function — this blog is an example of that in practice. Writing about it authentically is both content strategy and positioning.

**Content opportunities:**
- How we use Claude + Ahrefs + GSC to run content audits
- Our internal linking automation approach
- How to structure content for LLM citation

---

## Audiences (ranked by priority)

1. **Founders / Protocol leads** at series A–C crypto and fintech companies who need to communicate a complex technical product to a non-technical (but sophisticated) audience
2. **Heads of Marketing / Content** at established crypto companies who need external writing support at high standards
3. **Investors / Operators** evaluating content partners for portfolio companies

---

## Topics to avoid

- Generic "crypto explainer" content targeting retail investors (wrong audience)
- Anything that requires daily news reaction (not our model)
- Price predictions or market commentary (creates more risk than opportunity)
- Content that sounds like it could have been written by anyone in marketing (no point of view, no specificity)

---

## Competitive differentiation

Most crypto content agencies:
- Optimize for volume
- Have writers who learned crypto to write about it (vs. operated inside it)
- Treat narrative strategy and content production as the same thing

StoryArc:
- Prioritizes depth and precision over volume
- Founder (John) has operated inside Kraken, Babylon, Bitstamp — real insider credibility
- Treats narrative strategy as upstream of content: the positioning problem comes first, then the content

## Metrics that matter

**Business metrics (primary):**
- Inbound leads from organic search
- Inbound leads attributed to content (ask every prospect)
- Referrals from existing clients

**Content metrics (secondary, as leading indicators):**
- Organic traffic growth (GSC)
- AI referral traffic (ChatGPT, Perplexity in GA4)
- Topical authority score (internal tracking)
- Domain rating growth (Ahrefs, when connected)
