# StoryArc Strategies — Services & Capabilities Reference

## What StoryArc does

StoryArc is a narrative strategy firm for crypto and fintech companies. We help companies communicate more clearly, more consistently, and with a stronger point of view.

The core problem we solve: technical companies know too much about their own product. The inside language becomes vague externally. The gap between what they built and what the market understands is where growth gets lost.

---

## Services

### Positioning & Messaging
Clearer language. Sharper differentiation. Less filler.

**What it involves:**
- Messaging architecture (what to say, to whom, in what order)
- Value proposition clarity (not features — what changes for the buyer)
- Competitive differentiation (what makes this company's framing distinct)
- Founder voice development (making a technical person sound like a clear thinker, not a salesperson)

**Best for:** companies entering a new market, companies that have grown beyond their original messaging, pre-fundraise, post-pivot

**Not a fit for:** companies that need copywriters to fill a template

---

### Content & Editorial
Newsletters, educational content, launch messaging, long-form writing, executive voice, ongoing editorial direction.

**What it involves:**
- Editorial strategy (what to publish, how often, with what goal)
- Long-form articles (educational, thought leadership, explainer)
- Newsletters (institutional, community, product-focused)
- Executive ghostwriting (founder LinkedIn, op-eds, keynote narratives)
- Social content direction (X/Twitter, not mass scheduling)
- Learn Center / education hub buildout

**Best for:** companies that need consistent, high-quality content over time — not a one-off campaign

**Delivery:** ongoing retainer or project-based depending on scope

---

### Strategic Communication
Launches, fundraising, category creation, market positioning moments.

**What it involves:**
- Product launch narrative + content
- Fundraise messaging and investor-facing narrative
- Category creation strategy (positioning the company as the defining player, not a feature)
- Regulatory / market shift communication

**Best for:** high-stakes moments where generic content isn't good enough
